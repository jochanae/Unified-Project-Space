**Here's exactly what I'm establishing inside the workspace:**

**1. Problem Statement**
Fences are functional and ugly. Replacing them is expensive, disruptive, and often prohibited. No product currently solves the attachment problem elegantly across fence types. Verdure fills that gap.

**2. Customer Segments**
Three profiles fully built out — the homeowner upgrading their backyard, the renter who needs reversible transformation, and the commercial buyer turning outdoor seating into an experience.

**3. Positioning**
Not a fake plant product. An outdoor surface transformation system. Premium over the cheap Amazon category through attachment quality, installation experience, and material longevity.

**4. Attachment System Architecture**
The full mechanism landscape — universal clamp rail, magnetic attachment, interlocking clip spine, hybrid approach recommendation — with the MVP attachment spec called out specifically.

**5. Manufacturing Assumptions**
Supplier geography, MOQ expectations, UV stabilization standards, backing panel rigidity spec, and color consistency requirements.

**6. Competitive Landscape**
What exists, why it fails, and where the whitespace is. The category credibility problem as both a risk and an opportunity.

**7. Risks and Assumptions**
Wind load, cheap alternative competition, HOA restrictions, universal compatibility promise, color fading, and batch inconsistency.

**8. MVP Recommendation**
Wood privacy fence first. 8-panel kit. $120–$180 price point. Two foliage styles. All hardware included. No tools required. Validate premium willingness to pay before expanding fence compatibility.

**9. Future Expansion Opportunities**
Adapter kits by fence type, commercial grade line, retail distribution, complementary outdoor surface panels — sequenced and gated behind MVP validation, not premature.

**10. Open Questions**
Wind load engineering standards, supplier shortlist, HOA restriction prevalence in target markets, price sensitivity testing approach.
